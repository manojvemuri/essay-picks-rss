We Know Simple Fluids Can Flow. Turns Out, Some Can Fracture.

A simple nonelastic fluid cracked like a brittle solid, challenging a basic assumption about how liquids fracture.

Core idea

A nonelastic, apparently simple hydrocarbon fluid can crack like brittle glass under rapid stress. That observation undermines the familiar assumption that elasticity is what allows a fluid to fracture and revives a deeper possibility: fracture may arise from molecular cohesion, cavitation, and loading rate rather than elasticity alone.

Why it stands out

This is compact science writing at its best: an audible laboratory surprise leads into a genuine conceptual problem, then outward to unresolved theory and possible applications in fiber spinning, inkjet printing, brain-injury protection, and soft robotics. The result is more than a novelty report. It shows science correcting its categories when nature refuses to respect them.

The Stretch of a Soul

Biography and philosophy meet in an inquiry into what a human life can reveal beyond intellectual explanation.

Core idea

Reviewing Clare Carlisle’s Transcendence for Beginners, Shy asks what a human life can reveal that abstract argument cannot. Biography, encounter, devotion, and attention may enlarge philosophy because our primary relationship to the world is not exhausted by “knowing” it.

Why it stands out

This is criticism that becomes an inquiry in its own right. It neither dismisses mystical experience as irrational nor embalms it as a proposition to be inspected. Its durable question is methodological and personal: what forms of understanding become possible when another life moves us before we can explain why?

B-Sides: Albert O. Hirschman’s “The Passions and the Interests”

Why capitalism was once meant to tame destructive passions—and why prosperity can eventually weaken freedom.

Core idea

Capitalism was not originally defended simply as the most efficient route to wealth. Early modern thinkers promoted economic self-interest as a comparatively tame passion that might restrain political ambition and religious fanaticism. Hirschman’s darker twist is that commercial comfort can later weaken the desire for freedom.

Why it stands out

Daston distills a classic argument with exceptional economy and shows why it remains strategically useful. Markets do not merely allocate resources; they reshape motives and political character. The essay supplies a better lens for evaluating prosperity under illiberal government than the usual “growth versus values” framing.

‘It Is Comforting to Be Haunted’: How Attitudes to Abortion Have Changed Through the Ages

A personal and historical essay that restores bodily experience and circumstance to abstract arguments about abortion.

Core idea

Contemporary abortion language—rights, choice, life—often abstracts away the body and the long, uneven history of actual abortions. Maglaque joins her own experience to early-modern records to show that attitudes toward gestation, ensoulment, responsibility, pain, and loss were historically more contingent and complicated than today’s camps imply.

Why it stands out

The essay does difficult intellectual and moral work without flattening experience into a slogan. Personal testimony becomes a method of historical attention, while archival detail corrects myths about a single timeless religious or legal position. Its achievement is not false balance; it is restored particularity.

Inside the Luddite Festival Harnessing Gen Z’s Rage Against Big Tech

A grassroots experiment replaces platform dependence with offline events, analog tools, and community infrastructure.

Core idea

New York’s Summer of Ludd turns dissatisfaction with platforms into embodied experiments: phone-free events, zines, mending, shortwave radio, community expertise, and infrastructure that does not force social life through Big Tech. The modern Luddite position is less “anti-technology” than pro-autonomy and skeptical of rented infrastructure.

Why it stands out

Elliott reports the movement’s earnest fun, contradictions, and limits instead of using it as a quirky trend peg. The strategic insight is concrete: individual digital detox is fragile when everyone else remains on the platform; durable exit requires social alternatives and shared infrastructure.
